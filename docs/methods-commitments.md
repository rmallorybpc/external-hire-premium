# Methods Page Commitments: Draft from the Decisions Log

Date: 2026-06-13. Status: draft. Numbers computed from the worksheet on this date. Final numbers recompute after the Step 6 freeze. Every claim below traces to a dated entry in the decisions log (D-001 through D-073).

## The sample in one paragraph

The panel covers S&P 500 CEO transitions from 2010 through 2022. The backbone is the Gentry, Harrison, Quigley, and Boivie CEO Dismissal Database, version of October 10, 2024, filtered to point-in-time index constituents. The panel holds 589 classified transitions (432 internal, 157 external, 26.7 percent external) and 146 rule-excluded events, each exclusion carrying a named flag. Two rows are manual (Continental 2010, and Fastenal's Oberton-to-Florness 2016 surfaced in workstream 1), each added under a ratified rule with full 8-K provenance. Twenty-three parsed rows await classification in Step 6 workstream zero and may shift these counts slightly.

## The classification rule and why the threshold matters

A successor is internal with 12 or more continuous months of employment at the firm or a consolidated subsidiary at appointment. Board service is not employment. The threshold was pre-committed before classification began.

Three exhibits show the rule deciding cases by days:

- Campanelli (Endo): external by 5 days.
- Broussard (Humana): internal by no fewer than 2 days, bounded by his own employment agreement.
- Bisignano (Fiserv): external by 28 days.

The sensitivity ladder holds ten named, sourced cases spanning the contested range: Banga (10 months), Campanelli (11.8), Broussard (13), Hackett (14), Smits (15), Vestberg (16), Lobo (18), Easterbrook (21), Dickerson (22), Sachdev (24). The 24-month robustness check reclassifies the upper rungs. Spencer Stuart's 18-month insider-outsider convention reclassifies six sub-12-month externals (Vounatsos, Banks, Banga, Milligan, Campanelli, Bisignano) as internal. The same facts produce opposite classifications under different published conventions. That is the argument for pre-committing one.

## Exclusion taxonomy with counts

146 exclusions total. Change-of-control events: 92, including 10 take-privates. Interim spells: 24, each bridged to the permanent successor where one existed. Co-CEO arrangements: 10. Merger-of-equals with the CEO specified in deal documents: 11, each verified against the merger agreement or proxy, including one accelerated execution (Johnson Controls) where a forced exit still produced no hiring decision because the agreement had already made it. Source artifacts: 2. Duplicate-entity rows: 2.

The deal-term distinction (ratified, D-037): when an acquirer's board installs the acquired company's CEO, that is an external hire and stays in sample (2 cases: Saunders at Actavis, Pessina at Walgreens). When a merger of equals pre-writes the CEO, no hiring event occurred and the row is excluded (11 cases). The Duke-Progress coup and the JCI acceleration both stress-tested this line and both held.

## Data integrity statistics

- 15 chain-derived successor names were wrong and were corrected at review. Zero survived to a final classification. Chain reliability declines toward the window's end for a structural reason: the derivation requires the successor's own later departure.
- 2 source artifact rows were caught and excluded (a ghost entity row, a departure that never happened).
- The 2010 completeness sweep and the full-window rename-aware re-match recovered 80 transitions the index matcher had silently dropped, through two named leak mechanisms (current-name versus historical-name mismatch, and acquisition-exit ticker loss). Every cohort year gained rows.
- One famous case is absent by design: the Cliffs activist installation of 2014 postdates the firm's index removal, so the point-in-time rule excludes it.
- Five running-total errors in the log were corrected when computed counts disagreed with recalled ones. The corrections are visible in the log. Counts in this project are computed, not remembered.

## Verification ledger

Two tiers. VERIFIED means a primary or corporate source was checked in session (22 rows at the Step 5 close, concentrated in threshold and rule-decisive cases). PRELIMINARY means classified from the historical record with sources attached during the Step 6 per-firm pull (567 rows). No preliminary row enters analysis. Verification searches fire on conditions, not cadence: five of the final nine classification batches contained no case inside the 24-month zone and spent no searches.

## Named subgroups for findings

- Returning former CEOs: 14 flags across both poles. Continuous-employment returns (the Reese pattern, 7 cases) classify internal. Gap returns of any length (the amended boomerang rule) classify external. The subgroup reports separately.
- Boomerangs: 14, including a spinoff alumnus and a three-phase case (employee, non-employee, CEO) that the rule resolved without judgment.
- Board-insider externals: 34. Activist-context transitions: 23. Interim bridges: 43. Hired heirs: 24. Founders: 8. Exogenous-context successions: 16.
- Gender (descriptive only): 47 female successors of 589 (8.0 percent), 11 of them external. The share rises across the window and concentrates after 2019.
